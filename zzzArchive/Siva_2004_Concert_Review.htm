Carnatic Vocal Concert
by
Vijay Siva

 

 

CAMAGA's first major concert of 2004 was a tremendously enjoyable performance by Vijay Siva. Mr. Vijay Siva was ably supported on violin by R.K. Shreeramkumar and by J.Vaidyanathan on the Mridangam.

Mr. Vijay Siva is a popular artist in India and has traveled the world. A few from the audience who had not heard about him were amazed with his immense talent and ability. He was at ease singing in the high octaves. Many may not be aware that he was a child prodigy and had the ability to identify ragas by the age of four. He is a disciple of Sangita Kalanidhi D.K. Jayaraman. He also received guidance from D.K. Pattammal. Vijay Siva is also an accomplished Mridangist with traning from Kumbakonam Sri Rajappa Iyer.

 

The concert started with Vatapi Ganapathim in Hamsadhwani followed by a kruti in PurviKalyani. The main items of the concert were Nee Padamule in Bhiravi by PaTnam Subramanya Aiyyar. His imaginative alapanai was well matched by Shreeramkumar on the violin. Thani avardhanam by Vaidyanathan showed the talents of an equally capable young Mridangam artist. The other major item was a rare ragam tanam pallavi in Khamas. While these two items were pleasing, the rest of his concert was a great treat and the concert recording will be a welcome addition to one's music library.

Vijay Siva's Brovabarama in Bahudari was perfect. The audience was mesmerized by a very authentic sounding Bengali rendition Amar Janma Bhoomi in Yamuna Kalyani. I am sure Mr. Vijay Siva is fluent in Bengali to be able to render the lilting patriotic song with such passion. Vijay Siva's rendition of Eppo Varuvaro in Jaunpuri was preceded a wonderful series of Tamil viruttams in Mayamanava Gaula and Kapi which is still ringing in our ears. Equally enjoyable was his Desh based Purandara Dasa's Salagho Vittla.
