Great Composers' Day Celebrations

by Dr. SurySekar (ks.sekar@mgt.gatech.edu)

 

The carnatic music lovers of Atlanta and neighbouring areas celebrated the tenth anniversary of the Great Composers Day on Saturday, March 7, 1998, at the Hindu Temple of Atlanta in Riverdale, Georgia.  This day honors three great composers of carnatic music - the "Trinity" of Thyagaraja, Muthuswamy Dikshithar, and Shyama Sastri.

The day began at 9 a.m., with the group-singing of Thyagaraja's Pancharatna kirtanas in the ragas Naattai, Gowlai, Arabhi, Varali and Sriragam.  This was followed by the singing of the Trinity's compositions by young students of carnatic music in the Atlanta area.  After a 30-minute lunch break, the day continued with individual singing by adults.

A unique feature this year was a 45-minute presentation each by two senior musicians living in the US - Srimathi Janani Prabhakaran of Baton Rouge, Lousiana, and Srimathi Manjula Ramesh of Raleigh, North Carolina.  They were accompanied on the violin by Shri Lakshminarayanan of Miami, Florida, and on the mridangam by Dr. Ram Sriram of Atlanta.

The very successful day concluded a little before 8 p.m., after the group-singing of Thyagaraja's Utsava Sampradaya kirtanas.

This day also marked the formal launching of CAMAGA - the Carnatic Music Association of Georgia.  The response to the preliminary call for membership in this organization was very good, and it was announced that the "concert season" in Atlanta would begin with the Great Composers Day each year.  Thanks to the enthusiasm and support of carnatic music lovers not only in the Greater Atlanta Metro Area, but also from Athens and Augusta, Georgia, and from the nearby states of Alabama, Florida, Tennessee, North and South Carolinas, and Louisiana, as well as the encouragement and support from The Hindu Temple of Atlanta, this event is growing in success from one year to the next.
