Padma Bhushan Shri Trichur V. Ramachandran presented a well crafted, pleasing, three-hour Carnatic vocal concert on early evening April 24, 2004, at the Performing Arts Studio in Emory University, Atlanta, Georgia.  The concert was sponsored by the Carnatic Music Association of Georgia (CAMAGA). Accompanying Shri Ramachandran were Shri S. Ramakrishna on violin and Shri Ram SriRam on mirundangam.

 

Shri Ramachandran’s concert included an array of popular krithis as well as several compositions that have not been heard lately on the Atlanta concert stage.  After rendering a Varnam in Kambodhi composed by Swathi Thirunal and the Gowlai Krithi “Pranamamyaham”, Shri Ramachandran sang the Nattai krithi “Swaaminaatha Paripaalaiyaasumaam” at an enjoyable pace.  Raga Alapana in PoorviKalyani followed with the composition “Gnanamu Sagaradha”. The pace of the concert was increased by the lifting Kamas krithi “Seethapathe”. Shri Ramachandran demonstrated his skill in providing a balanced concert with the rendering of the solemn Bhiravi Swarajati “Kamakshi (Amba)”.

 

Shri Ramachandran is the senior disciple of one of the great doyens of Carnatic Music, Sangeetha Kalanidhi Shri G. N. Balasubramanian. The audience was thrilled when Shri Ramachandran presented GNB’s Hindolam composition “Sama Gana Lole” and Bahudari composition “Unnadiye Gathi Yendralaithen”.  The Hindolam Raga Alapana was vintage GNB.

 

Shri Ramachandran presented the Ritigowla krithi “Nannu Vidachi” before presenting centerpiece of the concert, “E Tavuna Ra” in Kalyani Ragam.  Shri Ramachandran presented a thorough exploration of the beauty of Kalyani in his masterful alapana. The challenging swara passages during the rendering of the krithi was vintage Trichur Ramachandran.

 

Shri Ramachandran concluded the concert with several lighter pieces, among which “Himagiri Thanaye” in Suddhadhanyasi and Subramanya Bharathi’s Ragamalika composition “Thikku Theriyatha Kattil” delighted the audience.

 

Shri Ramakrishna, a computer software professional based in Orlando, Florida, was superb in providing violin accompaniment.  Shri Ramakrishna was absolutely responsive to Shri Ramachandran during Shri Ramachandran’s presentation of Raga Alapanas and skillfully matched Shri Ramachandran during kalapana swara passages. 

 

Shri Ramakrishna is also a well trained vocalist, as evident by his flawless accompaniment during “Amba Kamakshi”.  Shri Ramakrishna simultaneous matching of the gamakas presented by Shri Ramachandran throughout the krithi greatly enhanced the soulful mood of the piece.

 

Shri SriRam provided solid laya support by unobtrusively preserving the kalapramanam of each krithi presented. Shri SriRam’s penchant for enhancing the sangatis offered by the vocalist shone during the Bahudari and Ritigowla pieces.  The intricate korvai presented during the taniavarthanam fittingly complemented the complex Kalayni kalpana swara passages rendered by Shri Ramachandran.  The teermanam at the conclusion of the taniavarthanam was well crafted and fed nicely to Shri Ramachandran and Shri Ramakrishna’s “pick-up” of the finish to the main item of the concert.

 

Favorable post-concert comments made by the rasikas attending the concert indicated that the three accomplished musicians presented a satisfying program in true CAMAGA tradition.
